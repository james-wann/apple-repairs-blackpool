## What's involved

Apple MacBook batteries are rated for around 1000 charge cycles. After 3 to 5 years of normal use, capacity falls to 60-70% of original, and what used to be a full day of battery life becomes 3 or 4 hours. A battery replacement restores the original full-day capacity.

We use genuine Apple cells, properly calibrated to the system after installation so the percentage reading is accurate from day one.

## How do you know it's the battery?

Hold Option and click the Apple menu → System Information → Power. Look at Cycle Count and Battery Condition. Cycle count above 1000, or any Condition status other than "Normal", means the battery is at end of life. Symptoms include: drops in big percentage jumps, doesn't last more than a few hours from 100%, the system feels sluggish under load.

## When this isn't the right fix

If the battery cycle count is low (under 300) but life is poor, the cause is more likely a software process keeping the system awake. Activity Monitor → Energy tab will show which. We diagnose this before fitting a new battery.
