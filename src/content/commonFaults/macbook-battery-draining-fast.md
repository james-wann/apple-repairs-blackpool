## What's happening

Apple batteries are rated for around 1000 charge cycles before their capacity drops noticeably. After 3 to 5 years of normal use, battery capacity falls to 60-70% of original. That's when day-long battery life turns into half-day.

You can check yours: hold **Option** and click the Apple menu → **System Information** → **Power**. Look at Cycle Count and Condition. Above 1000 cycles, or any condition status other than "Normal", indicates the battery is at end of life.

Software causes are also common: a runaway process keeping the CPU busy, the screen brightness pinned at maximum, or background sync (Photos library uploading, Time Machine running). These can chew through a battery in hours.

## What to do first

1. Check the battery cycle count (steps above). High cycles + degraded condition = replace.
2. Activity Monitor → Energy tab. Look for processes with high Energy Impact. If one app is sitting at 20+ Energy Impact when you're not actively using it, that's a battery killer.
3. Reduce screen brightness, disconnect external displays, and see if battery life recovers.
